Thank you for responding to the recent balcony and exterior elevated element inspection notice. California law requires periodic inspections of balconies, decks, stairways, and similar structures to ensure public safety.
Many California cities are mailing inspection notices. Confirm your local requirements and deadlines.
As a property owner or manager, you are required to ensure compliance with the following state statutes:
  • Complete required inspections within the designated timeframe
  • Document all findings and submit reports to appropriate authorities
  • Address any deficiencies identified during inspection
  • Maintain records for future compliance verification

Applicable Regulations

SB-721 (Apartments)

  • Applies to buildings with 3 or more rental units
  • Inspection required every 6 years
  • Covers balconies, decks, walkways, stairways, and railings
  • Must be performed by licensed inspector

SB-326 (Condominiums and HOAs)

  • Applies to common interest developments
  • Inspection required every 9 years
  • Covers load-bearing components 6+ feet above ground
  • Non-compliance may result in fines and liability
