Compliance Requirements

SB-721 (Apartments)

Applies to multi-family buildings with 3 or more rental units. Requires periodic inspections of exterior elevated elements.

  • Inspection every 6 years
  • Licensed inspector required
  • Repair timelines mandatory
  • Report retention required

SB-326 (Condos/HOAs)

Applies to common interest developments and homeowners associations. Covers elevated structures and common areas.

  • Inspection every 9 years
  • Association reporting required
  • Reserve study integration
  • Repair follow-through mandatory

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the training session free? ▼
Yes, the compliance training is completely free. It's a pre-recorded session with downloadable resources and compliance checklists.
What if my building is newer? ▼
Newer buildings may still have inspection requirements. Use our compliance checklist to determine your specific obligations based on construction date and building type.
How often are inspections required? ▼
SB-721 requires inspections every 6 years for apartment buildings. SB-326 requires inspections every 9 years for condominiums and HOAs, with the first inspection due by January 1, 2025.
What are the penalties for non-compliance? ▼
Cities can impose fines up to $500 per day for unresolved compliance issues. Non-compliance can also impact property financing, sales transactions, and insurance coverage.
